# Q30. I2C Protocol in IoT

## What is I2C?

I2C (Inter-Integrated Circuit) is a serial communication protocol used to connect multiple sensors and devices to a microcontroller using only two communication lines. It is commonly used in IoT systems because it reduces wiring and allows multiple devices to communicate on the same bus.

## What are SDA and SCL?

- **SDA (Serial Data Line):** Used to transfer data between the master and slave devices.
- **SCL (Serial Clock Line):** Provides the clock signal generated by the master device to synchronize communication.

## How does I2C Addressing Work?

Each I2C device has a unique 7-bit or 10-bit address. The master (such as an Arduino) sends the address of the desired slave device. Only the device with the matching address responds, while all other devices ignore the communication. This allows multiple sensors to share the same SDA and SCL lines.

## Three Common IoT Sensors that use I2C

1. BMP280 – Temperature and Pressure Sensor
2. MPU6050 – Accelerometer and Gyroscope
3. BME280 – Temperature, Humidity and Pressure Sensor

## Advantages of I2C

- Requires only two communication wires (SDA and SCL).
- Supports multiple devices on the same bus.
- Reduces wiring complexity.
- Widely used in embedded systems and IoT applications.
